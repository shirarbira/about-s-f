SHIRA ROSE FELDMAN

About me: I believe in the potential and promise of technology. For the past two and a half years I've been working in technology and development from a creative, editorial, and community-based role. I care deeply about our future and I care deeply for the cybernetic dream. I am passionate about contributing to bettering the current state of our technology-dominated culture, working to support an ethical future where privacy, civil liberties, and accountability are prioritized. I am seeking to facilitate and participate in these conversations and actions, particularly through strategizing, writing, creating, designing, programming and collaborating with others.

Core Experience

Executive Assistant to Taeyoon Choi, Co-founder of The School for Poetic Computation, Artist and Teacher

🌀Research + Development: Core research and editorial production for the ongoing series, The Distributed Web of Care, a technical and creative project about peer-to-peer networks and the distributed web. The project has been shown at The Internet Archive in San Francisco, The Whitney Museum, The New Museum, and Knockdown Center as in New York City.

🌀Coding + Editing: I also built and maintain the project website using Jekyll, and act as Editor for all published essays. Two pieces I particularly enjoyed working on are Mindy’s essay on building bespoke interfaces for digital archives + Shannon’s piece on designing accessible measures with creativity and care. Other writing responsibilities include grant-writing and other funding opportunities related to the project.

🌀Teaching: I’ve served as the Teaching Assistant for the course, Teaching as Art in NYU’s ITP department for now two semesters, helping to shape the class syllabus and weekly lectures and maintain the course website. I worked with students during office hours and provided feedback on homework and final projects.

🌀Organizing: A large part of my role working with Taeyoon has been helping with the production of large community events, including: Processing Community Day, Learning to Teach, and events held at The School for Poetic Computation and Data & Society Research Center.

Summer Fellow, Library Innovation Lab at Harvard University Law School

🌀Research + Production: Awarded a summer fellowship through the Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society to to support the production and publication of Network Play, a series of creative writings and computational works exploring the networked self in contemporary culture and our intimate relationships with technologies.

Creative Director, Civic Spirit

🌀Design: Served as head of design and creative production for Civic Spirit, a nonprofit project dedicated to educating and empowering middle and high school students in New York with a sense of democratic fluency and civic responsibility.

🌀Strategy + Branding: Co-organized Civic Spirit Day, a full day of gathering for 200+ teenagers from all Civic Lab schools together to connect and work collaboratively in designing solutions to civic issues. Responsible for all branding, merchandise, decals, banners, etc.
